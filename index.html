Lal salam, and welcome, dearest comrade

My birthname is John. I go by the pseudonyms ‘Holik’, ‘Feng Huang’, or the nom de guerre ‘Iron Phoenix’. This is my site.

I am a second-generation European settler born in and living in occupied KKKanada. My family are Scottish socialists, Palestinian nationalists, and Haitian refugees. We’re a fairly diverse family.

I am bound by an iron will, to fight arduously for fulfillment of humanity’s greatest historical mission: communist revolution. Come Hell, or high water—I will die for the revolution, because like the mighty phoenix, the revolution is immortal. Furthermore, I am dedicated in mind, in body, and in spirit, to the movement for national and cultural liberation of all colonized peoples from the KKKanadian settler state... and the construction of a socialist, Indigenous-led workers’ state.

I walk a shining path, the path of Marxism–Leninism–Maoism (M-L-M). I take on a very Third-Worldist approach. Moreover, my worldview is atheistic and historical-materialistic. “Read more, and self-criticize!” are good words to live by.

I wholeheartedly lead by example, by following the revolutionary lifestyle. I have scarcely any worldly possessions, merely my commonplace book, and my copy of Quotations. And my laptop. And my mic.

In my free time, I enjoy self-study (generally history/philosophy), self-defence/self-sufficiency, and music piracy.

Follow my Tumblr for micro-blogging and site updates. Follow me on Facebook for more personal stuff.

A Weekly, A Quarterly, And A Yearly

I do not hold my theoretical ability in high regard; Howbeit, I publish my thoughts weekly, quarterly, and yearly.

  • Weekly: Summarizing day-to-day struggles, reviewing other publications, outlining general Maoist theory and practice.
  • Quarterly: Analyzing Maoist theory and practice in-depthly.
  • Yearly: Summarizing the past year’s struggles, criticizing myself, reflecting on my weekly and quarterly work.

How To Strengthen Your Security

Practicing good security culture is critical to safeguarding revolutionaries and revolutionary organizations in the core, because of the power of the imperialist state. Be mindful that it is not Maoist practice to launch an armed struggle against an entity that has massive political and military might. Our objective is to weaken the state’s power over the people (non-militantly) and strengthen people’s power by building the subjective forces for socialist revolution (and national liberation) inside.

Here’s some advice for practicing good security culture:

  • Assume you’re being watched by CSEC, NSA, or GSHQ.
  • Don’t ask for, or give away personal, sensitive or incriminating information on you, a comrade, or an organization. Ever.
  • Don’t fall for agent provocateurs. Armed insurrection in the core is out-of-the-question at the present time.

The best security culture is communicating face-to-face and abstaining from Internet-based communication. That being said, here’s some advice for practicing good security culture on the Net:

  • Turn off your Wi-Fi or unplug your Internet when you are away from your keyboard.
  • Encrypt your emails. If you’re tech-savvy, set up GPG and use keys to transmit sensitive or incriminating information. Check out the site documentation for guidance. If you’re not tech-savvy, try on Freenet or Waste.
  • Use temporary email providers to transmit sensitive or incriminating information. Agencies can’t track the source.
  • Use public computers to transmit or view sensitive or incriminating information.
  • Hide behind a proxy. I recommend TOR. Do not torrent or open documents using TOR. Go offline to open documents.
    • Your IP address appears here. If that’s incorrect, disregard. If that’s correct, mask it.
  • Run your Firewall 24/7.
  • If you need to trade or store documents containing sensitive or incriminating information, ZIP and password the data, transfer it to a thumbdrive and give it to a comrade face-to-face.
  • Run CCleaner to obliterate all traces of recycled data.
  • Install a good security suite and fortify your computer against being spied on or hacked into.
  • Install anti-keylogging software, to encrypt your typing. I recommend Zemana.
  • Research further information.
