What Is Maoism Third-Worldism?

What Are The Classes Of Modern Society?

The Proletariat: Erroneously referred to as the “working-class”, or the “99%”. In Marxist political-economy, the proletariat are those of bourgeois society who satisfy their needs and wants wholly by selling their labour-power. By their labour-time, they produce use-values for capitalists, a meagre portion of which are given back. Use-values enter the market, and the proletariat has no control over their distribution and redistribution. More broadly, the proletariat encompasses the whole of the “toiling masses”, captive workers in the prison-industrial complex, immigrants, and colonized Indigenous peoples.

A majority of the proletariat toils under exploitation in the Third World. Workers of the First World are labour aristocracy.

According to Marxist historical-materialism, the proletariat is the revolutionary class of society. For the proletariat has a world to win by demolishing capitalism and constructing a socialist society of democratic, proletarian control over use-values.

The Lumpenproletariat: The lumpenproletariat are those of bourgeois society who would benefit materially from regular employment, even at the rates of exploitation. However, they are obstructed from participating in the legal economy, thus they are shackled to the illegal economy. The lumpenproletariat suffers greatly, as their labour-power cannot be sold. Oftentimes, they are forced to turn to crime to sustain their livelihood or families materially.

Maoism Third-Worldism regards the lumpenproletariat as the “cut teeth” of the revolution. Vanguardizing the lumpenproletariat is a strategic possibility. Furthermore, Maoism Third-Worldism regards the lumpenproletariat is the force best-suited for bringing revolution from the “countryside” (Third World) to the “cities” (First World).

A good modern example of a revolutionary lumpenproletarian is Comrade Sankiya Shakur. The Black Panther Party for Self-Defence was well-known for organizing lumpen, and Comrade Josef Stalin himself was a lumpenproletarian.

The Embourgeoisified Proletariat: The embourgeoisified proletariat are those of bourgeois society participating in the legal economy who, by their labour-power, do not produce any use-values. They are commonly referred to as an “aristocracy of labour”or the “labour nobility” as they form a class of power, and are not part of the toiling masses. The labour aristocracy are largely non-productive, thus wholly parasitic workers, a majority of whom are White, whose wages are super-saturated by the super-profit extracted from Third World production. Members of the labour aristocracy have more to gain by defending capitalist-imperialism than by fighting to demolish it. They have a decadent world to lose.

Generally, workers receiving more value than they produce are part of the embourgeoisified proletariat.

The Bourgeoisie: Erroneously referred to as the “1%”—they are, more correctly, those of society who directly hold or represent capital, thus weild economic power, thus are granted politico-legal and ideological power over society.

The bourgeois class is further divided by unique relationships to capitalist-imperialism.

The Petite-Bourgeoisie: Those of bourgeois society who try to mimic the haute-bourgeoisie, and may live partially by buying the labour-power of a worker, and selling the use-values therefrom. In the First World, alongside the shrinking of the proletariat, the petite-bourgeois class has shrunk. Bourgeois ideologues refer to it as the “death of the middle-class”.

The Haute-Bourgeoisie: Those of global society who directly hold or represent finance capital. They live wholly by exploitation of and domination over others, primarily the masses of the Third World.

The Comprador Bourgeoisie: Those of a colonial or neocolonial society who, freely or by force, “work under” the agenda of the global haute-bourgeoisie, as puppets to the interests of capitalist-imperialism.

The Lumpenbourgeoisie: Wealthy leaders of crime syndicates, cartels, mercenary paramilitaries, etc.—the bourgeois classes of the economic underworld.

Combat Liberalism... In Modern Tongue

Liberalism is the rejection of ideological struggle. Maoists embrace ideological struggle because it is an effective weapon for constructing and safeguarding communist unity. Liberalism is a Philistine, petty-bourgeois attitude that robs the ranks of their unity, organizational strength, defensive capacity, and discipline. Liberalism alienates the party core from the masses as well. Combating all manifestations of liberalism unifies and strengthens the masses and the party core.

Manifestations of liberalism include:

  • Letting disagreements “slide” in order to save face.
  • Criticizing a comrade’s mistakes behind their back.
  • “Playing it safe” to avoid the criticisms of a comrade.
  • Avoiding making criticisms of a comrade to “play it safe”.
  • Disobeying orders, thus undermining group discipline (and democratic-centralism).
  • Failing to report all counter-revolutionary behaviour. 
  • Making personal attacks.
  • Thinking of oneself as superior to the masses.
  • Thinking of oneself as superior to the party.
  • Carrying out revolutionary work, party work or mass work halfheartedly.
  • Having a liberal, Philistine attitude towards oneself.
  • Avoiding making self-criticisms.
  • Behaving non-communist generally.
